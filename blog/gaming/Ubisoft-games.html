Ubisoft Games on Sale: All The Best Deals!

In case you weren’t aware, the biggest shopping holiday of the year is right around the corner. Yes, Black Friday is nearly upon us, kicking off promptly following Thanksgiving in the US, which happens on Thursday, November 25th. And while some of the best deals are yet to come (don’t worry, we’ll have you covered), we’ve collected some of the best early Black Friday deals you can cash in on right now from various brands and retailers. But for now, here’s what is especially alluring in the deals world.


Section 1: Ubisoft Games on Sale



Ubisoft has released plenty of enticing deals in the past to keep you on your toes, and some of the best sales don’t hit until Black Friday itself. But with the deals now out in the open, we thought it was a good opportunity to do a comprehensive roundup of what’s available now.


Ghost Recon: Wildlands – $19 (PS4, Xbox One, PC)


Warner Bros. Games on Sale:

A variety of games from Warner Bros. Games are currently discounted on Amazon. You can score a new release title, like Mad Max and LEGO Harry Potter, while they’re on sale, or even add-on options like LEGO The Hobbit, LEGO Harry Potter, LEGO DC Superheroes, and LEGO Marvel’s Avengers, so you can build the perfect sports or shooter set for your budding teen.

Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas- $19.99

GTA 5 San Andreas currently has a sale price of $16.24 on Amazon, with the chance to get the game for $12.29 in the near future. The game typically sells for $19.99 on other online stores, with the added bonus of the game being DRM-free. Plus, for a game that was first released in 2004, it actually has a lot of modern polish to it, even with its pre-release roster of features.

Best Buy Deals:


Best Buy is celebrating Black Friday this year with a four day Black Friday sale, going from November 25th through November 29th, with up to 40% off many games. Deals include the PlayStation 4, Switch, Xbox One, and even a number of PC titles as well.

GameStop Deals:

While GameStop doesn’t quite have the same array of PlayStation, Nintendo, and Xbox One hardware on sale this year, the site is still one of the first to release Black Friday deals. The store has confirmed that they will be offering discounts on numerous games throughout the Black Friday period, and has already revealed some of the biggest sales.

Shovel Knight: Treasure Trove (PS4/Xbox One/PC) – $14.99

Everquest II (PS4/Xbox One/PC) – $39.99 & Darksiders II (PS4/Xbox One/PC) – $14 are also available at this rate.

GameStop Deals

For starters, GameStop has some solid deals on many hot exclusives. You can save upwards of $40 on Ubisoft titles like Rayman Legends, Beyond Good and Evil 2 and Assassin’s Creed Syndicate. These games aren’t normally in GameStop’s habit of carrying well-known software (which is why it’s so much more enticing to stock up on these titles) but GameStop is offering up the Xbox One and PS4 versions in all their respective formats for $20 (exclusively at GameStop) which includes these game’s must-have bonuses. If you’re looking for something a bit more streamlined, you can save 50% on select Xbox One and PlayStation 4 games from select publishers including Gears of War 4, Dead Rising 4, and Watch Dogs 2.

Target Deals

Of course, the biggest Black Friday game retailer on the planet is none other than Target, which will feature a whopping 50% off the following games (some titles are marked as “Black Friday Early Savings”): This game sale lasts until Wednesday, November 30th, which is a week after Black Friday on November 25th.

GoDaddy Deals

If you’re not using GoDaddy for hosting, but still use the company for other important things such as domain registration, you’ll be excited to hear that they’re offering 10% off of domain purchases, $10 off of a domain renewals and 25% off of domain transfers.

Nintendo Switch Bundles

If you’ve been lusting after Nintendo’s hybrid console, the Switch, but can’t quite swing the full $299 price tag for the portable unit, then check out these all-in-ones.The latest Switch bundle is an odd blend of design and functionality, and comes to you courtesy of GameStop. It comes with everything you need for the console, including an HDMI cable, battery pack, Joy-Con controllers, and even a carrying case, all packaged in a Nintendo branded case.
